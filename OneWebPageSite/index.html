5 Myths in Canberra debunked

Myth 1: Lake Burley Griffin fills up other ponds and lakes in New Zealand.

False

It is likely that water from Lake Burley Griffin has landed not only in ponds and lakes in New Zealand but also in the ocean and even in America due to evaporation. This theory initially started out with people believing that every time Lake Burley Griffin rises, a lake rises in New Zealand, but due to water being evaporated constantly when it rains, that water that was evaporated falls back down to earth, but there is no water stream that leads directly to ponds in New Zealand. Lake Burley Griffin only runs to the Molonglo River.

Myth 2:The missing bodies of Cadets from Duntroon are still in Lake Burley Griffin.

False

Five cadets were in a boat on Lake Burley Griffin and the boat capsized due to ‘rough waters'. Many people find it difficult to believe that these cadets drowned in rough waters when Lake Burley Griffin has very few waves as it and resides in the centre of Canberra. This brought the initial theory of there being a'monster' within Lake Burley Griffin as that was the only reason these cadets could have drowned in some not-so-rough waters. The police report has all five cadet bodies accounted for.
